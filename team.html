Raghav Bansal

Founding Partner

Raghav is a founding partner at Tesu Law, bringing over 8 years of experience in civil and commercial litigation, high-value contractual disputes, and arbitration. Before establishing Tesu Law, he honed his skills at some of India's most respected law firms, including Parekh & Co. and Trilegal.

During his career, Raghav has appeared in several high-profile matters before Constitution Benches of the Supreme Court. His experience includes appearances in landmark cases such as the Sabarimala Temple Review Petition, the challenge to the National Entrance cum Eligibility Test (NEET), the challenge to the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code 2016, and the challenge to the RBI circular on stressed assets.

Beyond litigation, Raghav frequently represents startups and investors in debt and equity transactions. He advises businesses on navigating complex regulations and high-stakes negotiations, helping companies structure deals that protect their interests while enabling growth. His ability to see both the legal and commercial dimensions of transactions makes him a trusted advisor to founders and investors alike.

Raghav's practice reflects his belief that effective legal counsel requires understanding not just the law, but the business context in which it operates. He brings this perspective to every engagement, whether arguing before the Supreme Court or negotiating a term sheet.

Pranav Butalia

Founding Partner

Pranav is a founding partner at Tesu Law with over 6 years of experience at Trilegal, one of India's premier full-service law firms. He graduated from the West Bengal National University of Juridical Sciences (NUJS), Kolkata, one of India's top national law schools.

Pranav specializes in drafting and negotiating high-value commercial contracts, with particular expertise in heavily-regulated sectors. His meticulous approach to contract drafting ensures that agreements not only protect his clients' legal interests but also support their commercial objectives. He has developed deep expertise in understanding how regulatory requirements intersect with business needs.

In addition to his transactional practice, Pranav is experienced in civil and commercial litigation. He frequently advises clients on the impact of court rulings on their businesses, helping them understand legal developments and adjust their strategies accordingly. This dual perspective, spanning both advisory and contentious work, enables him to draft agreements that anticipate and mitigate dispute risks.

Alongside his work at Tesu Law, Pranav is currently engaged with the World Bank on several of their projects, further expanding his expertise in development finance and institutional arrangements. This international experience enriches the counsel he provides to clients navigating complex regulatory and commercial landscapes.
